Project Detail

STARD-Net: SpatioTemporal Attention for Robust Detection of Tiny Airborne Objects from Moving Drones

Venue: ACM TSAS Publication Year: 2026 DOI: 10.1145/3787467
Back to Projects Code Demo Video Paper Presentation

Problem

Detecting tiny airborne objects from moving drone platforms is difficult because targets may occupy only a few pixels and appear under camera motion, clutter, camouflage, and partial occlusion.

Why It Matters

Reliable tiny-target perception is a prerequisite for robust UAV awareness, tracking continuity, and safety-oriented autonomous systems.

Method

STARD-Net combines spatial feature enhancement, temporal context modeling, and spatiotemporal attention for moving-drone tiny-object detection.

Key Technical Contributions

  • Spatiotemporal attention for tiny airborne object detection from moving drones.
  • Temporal context modeling for weak/faint target recovery.
  • Robustness under camera motion, clutter, camouflage, and partial occlusion.

Results / Impact

STARD-Net reports improved tiny-object detection reliability across challenging airborne datasets and maintains practical inference behavior.

Impact: Published in ACM Transactions on Spatial Algorithms and Systems, 2026.

Qualitative Comparison

Compared with frame-centric baselines, STARD-Net better preserves weak-target continuity in cluttered sequences and reduces misses for faint airborne objects.
