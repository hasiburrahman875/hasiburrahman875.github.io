STARD-Net: SpatioTemporal Attention for Robust Detection of Tiny Airborne Objects from Moving Drones

Venue: ACM TSAS 2026 Domain: UAV Perception Status: Published Tiny Target Detection Spatiotemporal Modeling
Back to Projects Paper Code Demo

Problem / Why It Matters

Tiny airborne objects in moving-drone videos are difficult to detect due to severe scale limits, motion-induced blur, cluttered backgrounds, camouflage, and intermittent occlusion.

Robust weak-target detection is critical for downstream airborne tracking, scene awareness, and reliability-focused UAV autonomy.

Method / Key Contributions

  • Spatiotemporal attention for tiny airborne object detection from moving drones
  • Temporal context modeling for weak/faint target recovery
  • Robustness under camera motion, clutter, camouflage, and partial occlusion

Results / Impact

STARD-Net is published in ACM Transactions on Spatial Algorithms and Systems (2026). See paper for full quantitative results across airborne object datasets.

Qualitative comparisons show stronger continuity for faint targets under clutter and moving-camera conditions compared with frame-centric alternatives.

My Role

I led problem framing, method design, implementation, experiments, and manuscript preparation under doctoral research supervision.
