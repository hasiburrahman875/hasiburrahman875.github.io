Project Detail

V-USDT: Vision-Based UAV Swarm Detection and Tracking by Leveraging Swarm Formation Constraints

Venue: IEEE MDM 2025 DOI: 10.1109/MDM65600.2025.00026 Type: Conference Paper
Back to Projects Code Demo Video Paper Presentation

Problem

UAV swarm tracking requires reliable identity association when targets are small, visually similar, and frequently occluded.

Why It Matters

Continuity failures in swarm tracking propagate to unreliable trajectory analysis and weak downstream autonomy decisions.

Method

V-USDT combines robust UAV detection with formation-aware association constraints to improve temporal consistency under weak and ambiguous observations.

Key Technical Contributions

  • Detection component tailored for small airborne targets.
  • Formation-aware constraints during multi-object association.
  • Integrated detection-tracking pipeline for swarm-level scenarios.

Results / Impact

MOTA

87.1 to 90.4

IDF1

91.1 to 93.1

Misses

78.24 to 63.29

Results are reported against baseline swarm tracking approaches without explicit formation-aware association.

Qualitative Comparison

V-USDT maintains stronger trajectory continuity during overlap and weak-target intervals than non-formation baselines.
