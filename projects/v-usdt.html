V-USDT: Vision-Based UAV Swarm Detection and Tracking by Leveraging Swarm Formation Constraints

Venue: IEEE MDM 2025 DOI: 10.1109/MDM65600.2025.00026 Type: Conference Paper
Back to Projects Code Demo Paper Presentation

Problem Statement

UAV swarm tracking requires stable identity association in scenes where targets are small, visually similar, and frequently occluded.

Research Gap

Appearance-only or short-term association methods can lose track continuity when swarm members overlap or when detections are weak. Formation structure is often underutilized.

Methodology

V-USDT combines a robust UAV detector with formation-aware swarm tracking to strengthen temporal association and reduce identity ambiguity during difficult tracking intervals.

  • Detection component designed for small airborne targets
  • Formation constraints used during multi-object association
  • Temporal consistency to stabilize tracking trajectories over time
  • Integrated detection + tracking pipeline for swarm-level scenarios

Results

MOTA

87.1 to 90.4

IDF1

Misses

Qualitative Comparison

Compared with tracking approaches that do not explicitly exploit swarm structure, V-USDT maintains stronger trajectory continuity during occlusion and weak-target phases.
