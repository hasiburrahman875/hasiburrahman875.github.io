Project Detail

KRAfT: Kalman Residual Diffusion with Formation Awareness for UAV Swarm Tracking

Status: Accepted Venue: IEEE ICPR 2026 Domain: UAV Swarm Tracking
Back to Projects Code Demo Video Paper Presentation

Problem

UAV swarm tracking can break under rapid motion changes, overlap, temporary disappearance, and weak visual observations.

Why It Matters

Stable swarm-level identity continuity is essential for behavior analysis, autonomy, and downstream decision support.

Method

KRAfT integrates residual motion refinement with formation-aware association and conservative recovery to reduce false target reattachment during missing observations.

Key Technical Contributions

  • Formation-aware UAV swarm tracking.
  • Kalman residual refinement for motion-consistent recovery.
  • Conservative recovery strategy to reduce false associations during missing observations.

Results / Impact

On UAV swarm benchmark settings, KRAfT reports HOTA values of 80.37, 70.59, and 65.31 across three test subsets, with evaluation compared against diffusion-style tracking baselines.

Dataset context and strict-win demo clips indicate better motion-consistent recovery and fewer unstable associations in difficult sequences.

Qualitative Comparison

Compared with diffusion-only baselines, KRAfT preserves formation structure more consistently and recovers missing targets with fewer aggressive false links.
