Domain-Generalized GeoAI

Domain-Generalized Multimodal GeoAI for Climate-Smart Agriculture

This emerging research direction extends my reliability-focused computer vision and multimodal learning work toward remote sensing and agricultural monitoring under domain shift. The goal is to study models that remain useful across unseen regions, years, climate regimes, and sensor conditions.

Area: GeoAI · Remote Sensing · Multimodal Learning · Domain Generalization Status: Emerging research direction
Research Context Discuss Collaboration
GeoAI Remote Sensing Domain Generalization Multimodal Learning

Problem Statement

Agricultural remote-sensing models may perform well under random train-test splits but fail when deployed in new geographies, seasons, crop calendars, climate regimes, or sensor conditions.

Motivation

Climate-smart agricultural monitoring needs models that generalize beyond the data distribution on which they were trained. This is important for crop mapping, stress monitoring, drought/flood impact assessment, irrigation analysis, and food-security decision support.

Methodology Plan

The research plan explores selective domain residualization and reliability-aware SAR-optical-weather fusion. The emphasis is on learning transferable crop and stress evidence while reducing dependence on unstable domain-specific shortcuts.

  • Model stable crop and land structure separately from nuisance domain information.
  • Use reliability-aware fusion across remote-sensing and environmental modalities.
  • Evaluate performance under realistic geographic, temporal, climate, and sensor shifts.

Target Data Modalities

SAR Sentinel-1 SAR for structure and moisture-related information.
Optical Sentinel-2 optical imagery for vegetation response and phenology.
Context Weather data and environmental covariates for climate and terrain context.

Evaluation Plan

  • Leave-region-out generalization.
  • Leave-year-out generalization.
  • Leave-climate-zone-out generalization.
  • Missing-modality and degraded-sensor stress tests.
  • Calibration, uncertainty, and worst-domain behavior analysis.

Current Status

This page presents an active research direction rather than a completed peer-reviewed project. Results, datasets, tables, and code links will be added only after they are ready to share.

Future Work

Future work includes implementing the evaluation protocol, comparing domain-generalization baselines, analyzing modality reliability, and developing interpretable indicators of geographic and temporal transfer.
