Project Detail

Radiogenomic Brain Tumor Classification from MRI Sequences

Domain: Biomedical AI Status: Translational/Secondary Direction Recognition: Top 3 Best Poster, Pathways 2026 Symposium
Back to Projects Poster Presentation Request Materials

Problem

MRI-based radiogenomic brain tumor classification aims to connect imaging patterns with biologically relevant tumor characterization.

Why It Matters

Reliable radiogenomic modeling can support clinically meaningful decision pathways in precision medicine contexts.

Method

This project explores multimodal MRI sequence modeling and translational classification workflows, informed by robust vision principles from UAV perception research.

Key Technical Contributions

  • MRI-based radiogenomic brain tumor classification workflow design.
  • Cross-domain transfer of robust multimodal learning ideas.
  • Poster-level translational communication for biomedical audience engagement.

Results / Impact

The work was recognized as a Top 3 Best Poster at the Pathways 2026 Symposium (NextGen Precision Health).

This project is an active translational direction; publication status is represented conservatively and aligned with currently available records.

Qualitative Comparison

Multimodal radiogenomic modeling is intended to provide richer context than unimodal workflows, especially when clinical evidence is subtle or heterogeneous.

My Role

I contributed to problem framing, modeling direction, experiment design, and poster presentation for this translational biomedical AI work.
