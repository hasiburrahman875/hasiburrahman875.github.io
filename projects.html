Uncertainty-Aware Multimodal UAV Tracking with Heterogeneous LiDAR Sensors

A track-first multimodal fusion framework for tiny airborne object tracking using sparse temporal tubes, transformer-based measurement prediction, uncertainty estimation, and EKF-style innovation gating.

Problem

Tiny UAVs produce sparse, unstable, and noisy observations across heterogeneous sensors. Direct raw point-cloud concatenation can fail when sensors have different fields of view, density, range, timing, and failure modes.

Method

The framework builds short temporal point tubes from heterogeneous sensors, predicts object existence and 3D correction using a sparse tube transformer-style model, estimates measurement uncertainty, and updates tracks through uncertainty-aware EKF-style filtering and innovation gating.

Key Contributions

  • Sparse temporal tube representation for tiny airborne object tracking
  • Heterogeneous multi-LiDAR sensing using sensors such as Livox Avia and Mid360/LiDAR360
  • Track-first fusion instead of raw point concatenation
  • Transformer-based measurement prediction with uncertainty estimation
  • Innovation-aware filtering to reduce catastrophic updates from unreliable observations
  • Real-world evaluation under sparse, noisy, and missing observations
