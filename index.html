Current Research Interest

domain-generalized multimodal GeoAI for climate-smart agriculture. My research interests focuses on building satellite-based AI models that can monitor crop condition, agricultural stress, and production risk across unseen regions, years, climate regimes, and sensor conditions.

The core problem I focus is that many remote-sensing models perform well under random train-test splits but fail in real deployment, where geography, crop calendars, weather, soil, clouds, and sensor conditions change. To address this, I am exploring selective domain residualization and reliability-aware SAR-optical-weather fusion.

My goal is to develop robust GeoAI systems that combine Sentinel-1 SAR, Sentinel-2 optical imagery, weather data, and environmental covariates for applications such as crop mapping, early crop stress detection, drought/flood impact monitoring, irrigation assessment, yield-risk estimation, and food-security decision support.

Research at a Glance

Primary identity: robust computer vision and multimodal AI for dynamic, uncertain environments. Core dissertation area: UAV perception, small-object detection, and swarm tracking. Extension areas: GeoAI and biomedical AI where robustness and generalization matter.

UAV Perception & Tracking

Robust small-object detection and swarm tracking under camera motion, clutter, weak appearance, and temporary target disappearance.

Multimodal Sensing & Reliable Fusion

Sensor-complementary learning and uncertainty-aware reasoning across visual, temporal, and 2D/3D sensing streams for reliable deployment.

GeoAI & Remote Sensing Generalization

Domain-generalized SAR-optical-weather modeling for climate-smart agriculture under geographic, temporal, and sensor shift.

Biomedical Imaging & Radiogenomics

Translational research direction on multimodal MRI-based radiogenomic classification and robust model behavior in clinically relevant settings.
