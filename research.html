Research

Robust perception in dynamic environments

My research asks how learning-based perception systems can remain accurate and temporally consistent when objects are small, observations are incomplete, and sensing conditions change.

Core themes

Small-object visual perception

I study airborne object detection from moving UAV cameras, where targets occupy few pixels and are easily confused with clutter. The work combines spatial and temporal evidence to improve robustness to motion, camouflage, and partial occlusion.

Representative work: STARD-Net

Spatiotemporal tracking and association

I develop methods for preserving object identity through ambiguous motion and missing detections. Current work investigates motion refinement, formation-aware association, and conservative recovery for visually similar UAVs.

Representative work: KRAfT and V-USDT

Reliable multimodal sensing

I am extending these ideas to heterogeneous LiDAR and multimodal data, with an emphasis on sparse observations, asynchronous sensors, uncertainty-aware fusion, and evaluation under distribution shift.

Ongoing work: MultiLiDAR UAV Sensing

Broader directions

The same reliability questions motivate my work in remote sensing and biomedical imaging: how should models behave when sensor characteristics, geography, acquisition conditions, or input modalities differ from training?
