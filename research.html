Research

Multimodal intelligence for reliable perception and health

My research develops reliable multimodal learning across visual, temporal, LiDAR, and biomedical imaging data, especially when observations are sparse, sensors disagree, or deployment conditions differ from training.

Core themes

Multimodal AI and reliable sensing

I study how models can combine heterogeneous evidence while remaining useful under missing inputs, asynchronous measurements, sensor degradation, and domain shift. Current work brings together visual, temporal, and sparse point-cloud signals for robust perception.

Ongoing work: MultiLiDAR UAV Sensing

Biomedical AI and radiogenomics

I extend reliable multimodal learning to biomedical imaging, with current work on MRI-based radiogenomic classification. The goal is to build models whose behavior remains interpretable and dependable across imaging sequences and acquisition conditions.

Related project: Radiogenomic MRI Classification

Computer vision and spatiotemporal tracking

I develop methods for detecting small airborne objects and preserving identity through ambiguous motion, clutter, occlusion, and missing detections. This work combines spatial evidence, temporal reasoning, and formation-aware association.

Representative work: STARD-Net, KRAfT, and V-USDT

Broader directions

The same reliability questions also motivate my work in remote sensing: how should models behave when geography, climate, acquisition conditions, or sensor characteristics differ from training?
