Research

My research centers on robust computer vision and multimodal AI for dynamic, uncertain environments. My dissertation work focuses on UAV perception, tiny airborne object detection, UAV swarm tracking, and spatiotemporal reasoning under clutter, motion, occlusion, missing observations, and sensor uncertainty. I also extend these reliability-driven ideas to GeoAI and biomedical AI, where models must generalize across domains, sensors, environments, and deployment conditions.

Core Area I: UAV Perception and Tiny Airborne Object Detection

My core dissertation work studies perception from a moving drone camera, where tiny airborne targets often provide only weak visual evidence.

  • Moving drone camera perception under scene motion and platform motion
  • Tiny airborne targets with low contrast and limited spatial evidence
  • Clutter, camouflage, occlusion, and faint target recovery
  • Spatiotemporal attention for sequence-level evidence aggregation

Representative work: STARD-Net, ACM TSAS 2026.

Core Area II: UAV Swarm Tracking and Temporal Association

I develop tracking methods for multi-object UAV settings where identities become ambiguous under overlap, missing detections, and coordinated motion.

  • Multi-object UAV tracking with visually similar airborne targets
  • Formation-aware association and trajectory continuity
  • Missing-observation recovery with conservative update strategies
  • Motion consistency for stable temporal reasoning

Representative work: KRAfT, IEEE ICPR 2026 accepted; V-USDT, IEEE MDM 2025.

Methodological Theme: Reliable Multimodal and Spatiotemporal AI

Across these projects, I am interested in methods that remain useful when the inputs are degraded, partial, noisy, or temporally inconsistent.

Uncertainty-Aware Inference

Model behavior should communicate reliability, not only predictions, especially under weak evidence and distribution shift.

Degraded Inputs and Missing Observations

Real systems need stable behavior when observations disappear, sensors degrade, or measurements arrive sparsely.

Deployment-Oriented Evaluation

I emphasize OOD/stress testing, sensor fusion reliability, calibration, and failure-mode analysis beyond clean benchmark splits.

Extension Direction: GeoAI and Remote Sensing Generalization

I am extending reliability-driven multimodal learning to domain-generalized GeoAI for climate-smart agriculture.

  • Sentinel-1 SAR, Sentinel-2 optical imagery, weather data, and environmental covariates
  • Leave-region, leave-year, and leave-climate-zone generalization
  • Reliability-aware SAR-optical-weather fusion under cloud, sensor, and domain shift
  • Applications in crop mapping, crop stress monitoring, yield-risk estimation, and food-security decision support

Extension Direction: Biomedical AI and Radiogenomics

My biomedical AI direction explores reliable multimodal learning for MRI-based radiogenomic classification and clinically relevant model behavior. This connects my broader interest in robust representation learning, multimodal fusion, and trustworthy decision support.

  • MRI-based radiogenomic brain tumor classification
  • Reliability and generalization under heterogeneous clinical data
  • Translational model behavior rather than benchmark-only optimization
