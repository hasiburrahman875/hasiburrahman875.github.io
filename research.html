Domain-Generalized Multimodal GeoAI for Climate-Smart Agriculture

My current research focuses on building trustworthy multimodal geospatial AI systems for climate-smart agriculture and environmental resilience. I am particularly interested in how satellite-based AI models can generalize beyond the regions, years, crop systems, and climate conditions on which they were trained.

Modern agricultural monitoring increasingly relies on Earth-observation data such as Sentinel-1 SAR, Sentinel-2 optical imagery, weather variables, terrain, and environmental covariates. These data sources provide complementary information about crop growth, vegetation condition, moisture, structure, and climate stress. However, agricultural landscapes are highly dynamic, and a model trained in one region or season may fail in another geography, year, or sensor condition.

This creates a fundamental out-of-distribution generalization problem in agricultural GeoAI. My work aims to address this gap by developing models that learn transferable agricultural signals rather than memorizing domain-specific shortcuts.

Research Problem

How can we build multimodal GeoAI models that reliably monitor crop condition, agricultural stress, and production risk across unseen regions, years, climate regimes, and sensor conditions?

Target applications include:

  • Crop type mapping
  • Early crop stress detection
  • Drought and flood impact monitoring
  • Irrigation and water-stress assessment
  • Yield-risk estimation
  • Food-security monitoring
  • Climate-resilient agricultural decision support

Methodological Direction: AgriShift

I am developing a framework tentatively called AgriShift, exploring selective domain residualization and reliability-aware multimodal fusion for agricultural remote sensing.

  1. Stable crop and land structure: persistent field, crop, terrain, and land-cover characteristics.
  2. Domain nuisance information: region-specific appearance, year-specific weather, sensor artifacts, cloud effects, and label-source bias.
  3. Transferable crop or stress evidence: signals that should remain useful across new geographies and seasons.

The objective is selective invariance: suppress unstable nuisance factors while preserving useful environmental context such as phenology and climate information.

Experimental Focus

The first stage investigates agricultural domain generalization on datasets such as CropHarvest using deployment-style splits.

  • Leave-country-out generalization
  • Leave-region-out generalization
  • Leave-year-out generalization
  • Leave-climate-zone-out generalization
  • Missing-modality and cloud-corruption stress tests

Evaluation emphasizes worst-domain performance, OOD gap, uncertainty, and robustness under degraded inputs.

Broader Vision

My long-term goal is robust, interpretable, and transferable GeoAI for real-world agricultural and environmental monitoring. I focus on systems that remain reliable in new regions, future seasons, climate extremes, and imperfect sensing conditions.
