Research Vision

My research centers on robust multimodal perception for sparse, noisy, and safety-critical sensing environments. I am particularly interested in making detection and tracking systems dependable under real-world constraints, including sensor mismatch, partial observations, and failure-prone conditions.

Core Themes

Theme 1: Robust UAV Perception

Detection and tracking of small airborne objects from moving platforms under occlusion, clutter, sparse observations, and domain shift.

Theme 2: Multimodal Sensor Fusion

Heterogeneous LiDAR/radar/vision fusion for real-world sensing, especially when sensors have different fields of view, density, range, failure modes, and timing.

Theme 3: Uncertainty-Aware Tracking

Track-first fusion, temporal tube modeling, transformer-based measurement prediction, EKF-style filtering, uncertainty estimation, and failure-aware sensor updates.

Theme 4: Translational Multimodal AI

Transfer of multimodal representation learning and uncertainty-aware modeling to biomedical imaging and remote sensing.

Future Directions

  • Calibration-free or weakly calibrated multimodal perception
  • Uncertainty-aware multi-sensor fusion
  • Tiny object tracking under sparse 3D sensing
  • UAV/remote-sensing foundation models for real-world deployment
  • Multimodal biomedical AI with uncertainty and clinical review
