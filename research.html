Research Vision

My research centers on robust detection, association, and temporal reasoning for small or weak targets in challenging environments. I work on methods that remain dependable under clutter, rapid motion, partial observability, and noisy measurements.

Core Research Themes

Spatiotemporal Modeling for Dynamic Visual Data

Designing models that jointly learn spatial context and temporal continuity to support stable tracking and interpretation over image sequences.

Small-Target and Weak-Signal Perception

Improving sensitivity to tiny airborne objects with limited visual evidence, especially in low-contrast, high-motion, and occlusion-heavy scenes.

UAV Swarm Detection and Multi-Object Tracking

Leveraging temporal association and formation-aware constraints to reduce identity switches, misses, and failure propagation in swarm-level tracking.

Multimodal and 2D/3D Sensing Pipelines

Building end-to-end experimentation pipelines across camera, LiDAR, and radar streams for reliable, deployment-oriented evaluation.

Methodology Style

Modeling Principles

  • Temporal consistency first, rather than frame-wise decisions only
  • Attention to weak cues and motion patterns in long sequences
  • Structured constraints (formation, geometry, gating) for robust association
  • Robustness over ideal-case benchmark optimization

Evaluation Principles

  • Stress-testing under occlusion, clutter, and missing observations
  • Reproducible protocols for dynamic 2D/3D data pipelines
  • Metrics aligned with deployment reliability, not only detection score
  • Qualitative diagnostics to explain model behavior in failure cases

Current and Near-Term Directions

  • Formation-aware diffusion and residual tracking methods for UAV swarms
  • Tiny airborne object detection with stronger temporal context integration
  • Cross-dataset generalization studies for robust low-altitude sensing
  • Deployment-oriented model validation in ROS2 and GPU-based environments
  • Translating reliable temporal perception ideas to broader dynamic data domains
