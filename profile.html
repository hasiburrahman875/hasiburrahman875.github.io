Who I Am

My journey moved from teaching Computer Science and Engineering in Bangladesh to doctoral research in the United States. Before my PhD, I worked as a lecturer and developed strong communication and mentoring skills. At Missouri S&T, under Dr. Sanjay Madria, my research evolved from UAV detection and tracking into multimodal sensing, uncertainty-aware modeling, and robust inference under sparse, noisy, and dynamic conditions.

Across UAV perception, GeoAI, and biomedical AI, the core theme of my work is reliability: building systems that stay useful when data is incomplete, uncertain, or shifted from training conditions.
